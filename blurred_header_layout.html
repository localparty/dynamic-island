Bitcoin currently relies on ECDSA cryptography that could be vulnerable to quantum computers, with experts estimating that 25% of all Bitcoin (worth about $500 billion) could be at risk due to exposed public keys.
Google's Willow chip, unveiled in December 2024, demonstrated quantum computing power that can solve calculations in five minutes that would take traditional supercomputers 10 septillion years.
The cryptocurrency community is divided on the timeline, with some experts like Adam Back believing the threat is 20 years away while others warn quantum advances combined with AI could accelerate the timeline significantly.
Current quantum-resistant algorithms like CRYSTALS-Dilithium and CRYSTALS-KYBER are being evaluated by NIST for post-quantum cryptographic standards.
Bitcoin developers are actively researching quantum-resistant signature schemes including hash-based signatures and lattice-based cryptography to future-proof the network.
The transition to quantum-resistant Bitcoin would require a hard fork and coordination across the entire network, making early preparation crucial.
Other cryptocurrencies like Ethereum are also exploring quantum-resistant upgrades as part of their long-term security roadmaps.
The quantum threat extends beyond Bitcoin to all current public key cryptography used in banking, secure communications, and internet infrastructure.
Companies like IBM, Google, and IonQ continue to make rapid advances in quantum computing hardware and error correction techniques.
The race between quantum computing development and quantum-resistant cryptography deployment has become a critical cybersecurity priority for governments worldwide.
